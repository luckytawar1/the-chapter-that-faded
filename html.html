The Chapter That Faded

There was a girl... not my girlfriend, not even someone I’d label clearly. I can’t reveal her name — not out of respect, but because I know some names are better left unspoken.

She wasn’t someone I was in love with, but she did have a small space somewhere in the chaos of my mind. A quiet type, reserved, maybe a little moody — the kind you can’t really read easily.

Our chats weren’t extraordinary, but they weren’t meaningless either. Somewhere in between casual jokes and personal thoughts, something settled — not exactly a bond, but not ignorance either.

We started talking about random things — life, overthinking, the stupid situations school throws at us. At times, she replied late. Sometimes, she ignored my lines completely. But I got used to it.

Once in a while, she'd open up — like peeling one layer from a locked box. I didn’t try too hard, because I knew if I pulled more, she'd disappear.

She had her walls. I had my curiosity. She gave one-liners. I typed paragraphs. Classic imbalance, but still… it worked in its own weird way.

Time passed. Conversations got dry. Replies turned into reactions. What once felt like a vibe now started feeling like a silence wearing makeup.

I never confessed anything. Neither did she. We never labeled what this was — and maybe that’s why it faded without needing to end.

No big drama. No final goodbye. Just gaps… then distance… then nothing.

Sometimes, people come into your life like passing clouds — they don't stay, but they do shade you for a moment.

This wasn't a love story. This was just a chapter that faded.
